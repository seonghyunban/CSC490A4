\subsection{Reward Design}

All additional rewards are designed to complement the original binary correctness reward rather than replace it.
Each reward maps a model response to a scalar in $[0, 1]$, and rewards are summed during advantage computation.
Rewards A and B target non-overlapping failure modes identified from the SFT baseline: A addresses responses lacking a parseable answer format, while B provides partial credit for formatted-but-wrong answers.
Rewards C, D, and E are designed from exploratory data analysis of the pre-P3 RL runs, which revealed that 82\% of ``correct'' responses are reward hacking---solving a different problem that coincidentally produces the right number.
C addresses text degeneration (32\% gibberish), while D and E enforce input grounding by checking entity and number overlap between question and response.
All five rewards are rule-based with no learned components, following the principle advocated by DeepSeek-R1 (2025) that verifiable rewards resist reward hacking better than neural reward models.
