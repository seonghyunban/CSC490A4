\subsubsection{Reward B: Numeric Proximity}
\nestindent

\textbf{What it is.}
A continuous reward providing partial credit based on the distance between predicted and gold answers:
\[
R_B(\text{pred}, \text{gold}) = \max\!\left(0,\; 1 - \frac{|\text{pred} - \text{gold}|}{|\text{gold}| + 1}\right)
\]
If the response contains no parseable number, $R_B = 0.0$.
The denominator $|\text{gold}| + 1$ normalizes distance relative to answer magnitude and prevents division by zero.
A score of 0.95 means the prediction is within 5\% of gold (e.g., predicting 95 when gold is 100); 0.0 means either no answer was extracted or the prediction is far from gold.

\textbf{Why we chose it.}
To address the 8.5\% near-miss rate identified in Section~1.2: under binary correctness, a response off by 1 is treated identically to one off by 10{,}000.
Proximity provides graded signal proportional to closeness, creating advantage variance among the 16 samples even when none are exactly correct.

Distance-based partial credit is grounded in the reward shaping framework of Ng et al.~\cite{ng1999reward}.
Unsloth's RL documentation~\cite{unsloth} recommends proximity-based rewards for math tasks when exact-match rates are low.
