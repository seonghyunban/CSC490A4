We designed five additional reward functions for GSM8K RL training, targeting format compliance (A), numeric proximity (B), text coherence (C), entity grounding (D), and number grounding (E).
Number grounding (E) proved the most effective individual reward (+4.1pp Pass@1), followed by coherence (C, +3.2pp) and format compliance (A, +2.6pp), while numeric proximity (B, +0.7pp) fell below the noise threshold.
The combined run with all five rewards achieved the strongest overall improvement (+4.9pp Pass@1, from 9.7\% to 14.6\%), with extraction failures dropping from 60.3\% to 20.0\%.
The C+D+E combination achieved the highest Pass@8 (39.1\%) and lowest extraction failure rate (15.9\%) of any configuration, though with a wider consistency gap.
Error analysis revealed a consistent structural shift: format-related failures decreased from 70.7\% to 12.7\% of errors in the combined run, while reasoning failures (wrong setup, arithmetic error) rose to dominate at 62.5\%, exposing the underlying reasoning bottleneck.
Exploratory data analysis uncovered pervasive reward hacking---82\% of ``correct'' baseline responses solve a different problem by coincidence---motivating the grounding rewards that directly penalize input-unfaithful responses.
The rewards composed constructively in combination, with gains scaling with reward count while losses remained stable at 44--55 problems, suggesting that additional orthogonal reward signals yield diminishing but positive returns.
