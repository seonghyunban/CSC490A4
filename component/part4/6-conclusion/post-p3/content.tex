We designed five additional reward functions for GSM8K RL training, targeting format compliance (A), numeric proximity (B), surface non-degeneracy (C), entity grounding (D), and number grounding (E).
Number grounding (E) proved the most effective individual reward (\pFourSeparateEDeltaPassOne{}pp Pass@1), followed by non-degeneracy (C, \pFourSeparateCDeltaPassOne{}pp) and format compliance (A, \pFourSeparateADeltaPassOne{}pp), while numeric proximity (B, \pFourSeparateBDeltaPassOne{}pp) fell below the noise threshold.
The Comb.\ All run (all five rewards) achieved the strongest overall improvement (\pFourCombinedAllDeltaPassOne{}pp Pass@1, from \pFourBaselinePassOne\% to \pFourCombinedAllPassOne\%), with extraction failures dropping from \pFourBaselineExtrFail\% to \pFourCombinedAllExtrFail\%.
The C+D+E combination achieved the highest Pass@8 (\pFourCombinedCdePassEight\%) and lowest extraction failure rate (\pFourCombinedCdeExtrFail\%) of any configuration, though with a wider consistency gap.
Error analysis revealed a consistent structural shift: format-related failures decreased from \pFourBaselineFmtFailPct\% to \pFourCombinedAllFmtFailPct\% of errors in the Comb.\ All run, while reasoning failures (large error, arithmetic error) rose to dominate at \pFourCombinedAllReasonPct\%, exposing the underlying reasoning bottleneck.
Exploratory data analysis uncovered pervasive reward hacking---82\% of ``correct'' baseline responses solve a different problem by coincidence---motivating the grounding rewards that directly penalize input-unfaithful responses.
The rewards composed constructively in combination, with gains scaling with reward count while losses remained stable at \pFourLossMin--\pFourLossMax{} problems, suggesting that additional orthogonal reward signals yield diminishing but positive returns.
